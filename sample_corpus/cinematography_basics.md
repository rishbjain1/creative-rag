# Cinematography Basics (sample corpus)

Public, generic notes so the container is self-contained for a deploy demo.
The real index is built from a private corpus via `CRAG_CORPUS_ROOT`; this file
ships only to make the deployed image answer something out of the box.

## Film stocks
- Kodak Vision3 250D — daylight-balanced, fine grain, natural color. Good for day exteriors.
- Kodak Vision3 500T — tungsten-balanced, warmer, holds shadow detail at night.
- Cinestill 800T — tungsten-balanced, teal/blue shadows with warm halation in highlights; very cinematic for night and low light.
- Kodak Portra 400 — warm, golden, flattering skin tones; a strong portrait stock.

## Lenses
- 14–24mm — wide establishing shots, deep focus, environmental context.
- 35mm — natural, documentary feel, close to human-eye perspective.
- 50mm — neutral standard, versatile.
- 85mm f/1.4 — portraits and face close-ups; razor-sharp subject with creamy background separation.
- 100mm macro — insert and detail shots (hands, props, texture).

## Lighting
- Backlight / contre-jour places the key behind the subject for rim light and atmosphere; camera sits on the shadow side.
- Golden hour gives warm, low, directional light with long shadows.
- Practical-only lighting (lamps, windows, signage that exist in the scene) reads as natural; avoid unmotivated studio fill.
- Soft light (large source, diffused) flatters skin; hard light (small source) adds contrast and drama.

## Finish
- Fine-film texture (grain, halation, shallow depth of field) reads as photographed; clean digital reads as plastic.
- Shoot for an untreated photographic look; avoid heavy digital regrading and LUTs that flatten the image.
